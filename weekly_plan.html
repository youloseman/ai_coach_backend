3) High‑level periodization until 2026‑05‑24

We’ll treat now as late 2025 and plan to race in May 2026.

Macro‑phases:
1. Base 1 (Nov 2025 – Feb 2026) – ~16 weeks
2. Build 1 (Mar – mid‑May 2026) – ~10 weeks
3. Peak (late May 2026) – ~2 weeks
4. Taper (final 10–14 days pre‑race – overlaps with Peak)

Because the race is still far, we can also think in two macro‑cycles:
- Cycle A: Now → Jan 2026: 10K focus + general tri base, heavy on swim and aerobic work.
- Cycle B: Feb → May 2026: 70.3‑specific build, peak, taper.

Detailed outline:

Phase 1: Base 1 / General Prep (Now – end Feb 2026, ~16 weeks)
Primary aims:
- Establish consistent triathlon routine: 3x swim, 3–4x bike, 3–4x run per week.
- Fix intensity distribution: 80–90% of sessions easy, 10–20% quality.
- Build swim volume and technique.
- Maintain and slightly sharpen run for the 10K attempt without overdoing intensity.
- Build bike aerobic base and some low‑intensity strength (hills/big gear).

Key elements:
- Running:
- 1 quality session/week (alternating: tempo/threshold intervals, short hill reps).
- 1 long run (easy, HR clearly lower than current – think conversational).
- 1–2 easy runs (shorter, pure Z1–Z2).
- Cycling:
- 2–3 aerobic rides (60–120 min, mostly Z2).
- 1 light interval session (e.g., sweet spot/tempo blocks) every 1–2 weeks.
- Swimming:
- Start with 2x/week, progress to 3x/week.
- Focus on drills + short intervals (e.g., 25–100m repeats) to build technique and comfort.
- Strength:
- 1–2x/week short functional strength/core (can be adapted from your CrossFit background but controlled, not maximal).

Within this phase:
- Early (Nov–Dec): emphasize technique, easy volume, and getting used to 3‑sport weeks.
- Dec–Jan: add more structured run intervals to give you a shot at a 10K PB.
- After 10K race (mid‑Jan): shift emphasis from pure run speed to tri‑specific durability and swim/bike volume.

Phase 2: Build 1 / 70.3‑specific (Mar – mid‑May 2026, ~10 weeks)
Primary aims:
- Increase race‑specific intensity and brick work.
- Solidify swim endurance at race pace.
- Raise bike FTP and ability to ride long at ~70–80% of FTP.
- Improve run off the bike (bricks, tempo runs).

Key elements:
- Running:
- 1 threshold/tempo session per week (e.g., 3×10 min @ ~HM pace, or 5–8 km continuous tempo).
- 1 long run (up to 20–24 km, mostly easy, occasional last 5–8 km at 70.3 pace).
- 1 short brick run off the bike (20–40 min at 70.3 run pace) most weeks.
- Cycling:
- 1–2 quality sessions/week:
- e.g., 3×15–20 min @ sweet spot / low threshold,
- or 4×8–10 min @ threshold.
- 1 long ride (2.5–3.5 h) with extended blocks at 70.3 power/effort.
- Some bricks with race‑pace segments.
- Swimming:
- 3x/week:
- 1 technique + drills,
- 1 threshold/VO2 (e.g., 10×100m @ strong but sustainable),
- 1 endurance (e.g., 4×400m or 3×600m at steady pace).

Phase 3: Peak (late May 2026, ~2 weeks)
Primary aims:
- Sharpen race‑specific intensity.
- Maintain volume but slightly reduced, with more rest.
- Practice race nutrition and pacing in a few key sessions.

Key elements:
- 1–2 key brick workouts simulating race intensity but shorter (e.g., 2 h bike with 3×20 min at race effort + 8–10 km run at race pace).
- Swim sets with longer continuous efforts at race pace (e.g., 1×1500m steady).
- Overall volume down ~20–30% from peak, but intensity maintained.

Phase 4: Taper (last 10–14 days pre‑race)
Primary aims:
- Arrive fresh, not flat.
- Maintain feel for race pace in all three sports.

Key elements:
- Reduce volume to ~40–60% of peak by race week.
- Short, sharp efforts at race pace in all disciplines.
- Extra sleep, dialed nutrition, no new stressors.

4) Weekly focus & approximate hours per phase

Given your 8–10 h/week availability, we’ll keep most weeks in that band, with a few 10–11 h peaks.

Phase 1: Base 1 / General Prep (Now – end Feb 2026)

Typical week: 8–9 h
- Swim: 2–3x (total 1.5–2.5 h)
- Bike: 3x (total 3–4 h)
- Run: 3–4x (total 3–3.5 h)

Weekly focus:

- Build consistent swim habit and gradually reach ~5–6 km/week.
- Run: keep one quality session + one long run, but slow down most other runs.

Phase 2: Build 1 / 70.3‑specific (Mar – mid‑May 2026)

Typical week: 9–10.5 h (some peak weeks up to ~11 h)
- Swim: 3x (2–3 h, ~6–7 km/week).
- Bike: 3–4x (4–5 h, including one long ride and one interval session).
- Run: 3–4x (3–3.5 h, including one tempo/threshold, one long, one brick).
- Strength: 1x 30–40 min maintenance.

Weekly focus:
- 1–2 key sessions per sport each week; everything else supports recovery.
- Regular bricks to condition your legs to running off the bike.
- Practice race nutrition on long rides and long bricks.

Phase 3: Peak (late May 2026, ~2 weeks)

Typical week: 7–8 h
- Slightly reduced volume but maintain intensity.
- 1–2 key race‑specific bricks and one key swim set per week.
- More rest days or very light days.

Phase 4: Taper (final 10–14 days)

Race week: 5–6 h
- Short, specific sessions in all three sports.
- No heavy strength work.
- Focus on feeling sharp and rested.
